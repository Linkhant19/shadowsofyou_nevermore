Nevermore

Whispers. Timing.
Silence. Crying.
The clock strikes twelve.
One more shot
Still won’t make sleep come to me.

Shadows. Breathing.
Helpless. Screaming.
The clock strikes twelve again.
One more shot
Still won’t make you come back to me.

So, I pick up my phone and call my friend
Just to hang up before they pick up.

No one wants to know you are breaking down
No one needs to know you are thinking of leaving forever
No one cares about your fears, tears, or darkest thoughts
No one, I mean no one, loves a broken dog.
So, I tell myself, “Please stop. I don’t want to lose them all.
This pain is not forever, so keep it locked up and
they will stay forevermore.”

Memories. Drowning.
Blood. Punching.
The clock strikes four.
One more shot
Still won’t make these painful thoughts go away.

So, I pick up my phone and text my friend
Just to ignore them when they reply, “What’s up?”

No one wants to know you are breaking down
No one needs to know you are thinking of leaving forever
No one cares about your fears, tears, or darkest thoughts
No one, I mean no one, loves a broken dog.
So, I tell myself, “Please stop. I don’t want to lose them all.
This pain is not forever, so keep your mouth shut and
they will stay forevermore.”

Flashback to a boy in a playground
Watching all the other kids playing from afar
With tears in his eyes
He just stood alone, wishing to leave that town
So, you are not gonna destroy it now.

Flashback to a boy in front of his computer
Waiting for all his so-called friends to come online
With tears in his eyes
Staring at the video game's main screen till dawn,
He promised himself, and said, “Nevermore”

He picked up a board game.
He picked up a smile.
He learned to entertain.
He learned to initiate
Conversations, parties, events, and hangouts,
And to make a fool of himself to make others laugh,
And that a pain well hidden is the secret to making any relationship last.

(No one wants to know you are breaking down
No one needs to know you are thinking of leaving forever)

He cried after every event and party he hosted
But, no one noticed. Nevermore.

He practiced speeches to tell his friend how he felt ignored,
but he stayed silent. Nevermore.

He almost made a scene with a friend who broke promises,
but he learned to leave without slamming the door. Nevermore.

His heart broke every time he saw a man and a woman merrily in love,
reminding him of a life he may never lead.
But now, he just laughs it out with his friends about
how annoying couples are. Nevermore.

No one cares about a broken dog. Nevermore.
